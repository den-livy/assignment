45-min commute with laptop, documents, lunch. Previously had rigid bottle leak on iPad. Now carries collapsed bottle in outer pocket—expands at office water cooler. Clips to bag strap when full.
THE STUDENT
[SKETCH/PHOTO: Library desk scene
laptop, textbooks, bottle beside notebook,
studying environment]
James, 21, Engineering Student
Library sessions with 15" laptop, 3 textbooks, calculator. Every ounce matters. Bottle stays collapsed until needed, then sits beside laptop without fear of leaks. Transparent cap shows when refill is needed.
THE GYM-GOER
[SKETCH/PHOTO: Gym bag open showing
minimal contents: towel, shoes, collapsed bottle,
keys, phone]
Alex, 29, Fitness Enthusiast
Minimalist gym bag philosophy. Bottle arrives empty and collapsed (saves space), fills at gym fountain, stays secure during workout. Easy wipe-clean exterior handles sweat. All parts detach for dishwasher.
THE TRAVELER
[SKETCH/PHOTO: Airport security scene
or carry-on luggage with collapsed bottle
taking minimal space]
Maria, 34, Consultant
Weekly flights, strict carry-on limits. Bottle passes through airport security empty and flat (takes up less space than a phone). Fills after security. Magnetic closure never accidentally opens in bag during turbulence.

Key Insight: The bottle's value isn't just in its collapsed state—it's in the flexibility to adapt throughout the day. Full when needed, compact when not. This mirrors modern life: we move between contexts constantly and need objects that move with us.

Step 3: Dry & Reassemble
Air dry fully (no hidden crevices), snap back together
Solving the "Gross Bottle" Problem: Traditional water bottles develop odors because users can't reach inside to clean properly. Our modular design eliminates all hidden spaces—every surface is accessible. The detachable grip ring is particularly crucial: this is where residue typically accumulates but is usually impossible to clean.
