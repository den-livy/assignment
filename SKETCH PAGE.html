Exploration: Multiple Bottle Body Concepts
Concept A: Vertical Accordion
[DRAW: Bottle with horizontal folds stacked vertically - 4 folds]
✓ Most compact when collapsed
✓ Stable structure when full
→ SELECTED for development
Concept B: Telescoping Segments
[DRAW: Bottle with rigid segments that slide into each other]
✓ Very stable
✗ Heavier (rigid materials)
✗ Hard to clean between segments
Concept C: Twist-Collapse
[DRAW: Bottle with spiral fold pattern]
✓ Interesting mechanism
✗ Too complex to manufacture
✗ Difficult to use one-handed
Concept D: Flexible Pouch
[DRAW: Soft bag-style bottle, no structure]
✓ Ultra-lightweight
✗ No structure when full
✗ Hard to drink from
Concept E: Origami Fold
[DRAW: Bottle with angular geometric folds]
✓ Visually unique
✗ Sharp edges uncomfortable
✗ Stress points would fail
Concept F: Roll-Up Design
[DRAW: Bottle that rolls from bottom like toothpaste]
✓ Simple mechanism
✗ Awkward to use
✗ Can't set down when rolled
DECISION: Vertical accordion (Concept A) provides the best balance of compactness, stability, ease of use, and cleanability. The horizontal folds allow natural compression while maintaining circular cross-section for comfortable drinking.
Lid & Closure Mechanism Exploration
Cap Options (draw side-by-side comparisons)
[DRAW 4 different lid designs:]
1. Screw cap (traditional threads)
2. Flip-top sport cap
3. Magnetic snap closure ✓ SELECTED
4. Push-pull silicone nozzle
Why magnetic closure?
• No threads = easier to clean (no trapped residue)
• Strong seal prevents leaks (neodymium magnets)
• One-handed operation (snap on/off)
• Satisfying tactile feedback when closing
• Transparent cap shows fill level and cleanliness
